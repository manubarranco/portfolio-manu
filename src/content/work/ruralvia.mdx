## The challenge

The challenge at RSI, a company within the Caja Rural Group serving over 70 financial entities, was to redesign the Ruralvía online banking app, focussing on the user. The goal was to significantly improve user interaction with mobile banking, while maintaining the closeness and trust characteristic of the Cajas Rurales. The mission involved not only creating an attractive and functional interface but also ensuring a smooth and efficient user experience that reflected the essence of the banking relationship in rural communities.

## My role

As the leader of the UX-UI design team at RSI for the Ruralvía app project, my role covered the full design process:

- Facilitating workshops to brainstorm and validate concepts with stakeholders.
- Conducting quantitative and qualitative research to understand user needs and behaviors.
- Conceptualizing, designing, and prototyping the app.
- Leading usability tests with real users.
- Presenting and defending design decisions to stakeholders.
- Developing a proto-design system, including design tokens and standardization of components.
- Creating UI component libraries in Sketch.

## Understanding the user

We started before any screen was drawn. The first step was understanding who was actually using Ruralvía (and who wasn't using it as much as they could).

We ran a quantitative survey with 201 responses, desk research including app store reviews and industry reports, and five in-depth interviews with real users across different regions of Spain: a 28-year-old single professional in Granada, a couple in their 40s in Asturias, a young woman in Segovia, a family in Colmenar Viejo, and a freelancer in Madrid.

<figure class="shot"><img src="/work/ruralvia/research-photo.webp" alt="Research and concept sketching" loading="lazy" /><figcaption>Mapping the experience by hand before any screen</figcaption></figure>

The picture that emerged was consistent. People used their banking app for two things: checking their balance and making transfers. Everything else (expense reports, savings tools, financial products) was either ignored, confusing, or simply not trusted.

<figure class="shot"><img src="/work/ruralvia/persona-board.webp" alt="User research synthesis board" loading="lazy" /><figcaption>Synthesizing what users say, do, think and feel</figcaption></figure>

> The Caja Rural app talks Greek to me. Financial terms I just don't understand.

Users didn't need more features. They needed an expert they could trust.

## Five insights. One direction.

The research produced five clear insights. Some confirmed what the team suspected. Others reframed the problem entirely.

Users found banking products boring and irrelevant unless they addressed a concrete need right now. They wanted their finances to feel personal (like Netflix recommending based on what you actually watch, not a generic catalogue). They asked for expense reports but only used them when the categorization was accurate and the interaction was simple. When it wasn't, they abandoned the feature.

But two insights stood above the rest. The first was about Caja Rural's core asset: closeness. Users trusted Caja Rural precisely because it felt human. They knew their advisor by name. Their advisor knew their family. That trust was real, and rare in banking. The question was whether it could survive the move to mobile.

The second reframed everything else:

> Call me. Tell me how to invest my money and make it work for me. That's when I'd think: these people actually care.

Users weren't asking for a better interface. They were asking for someone in their corner.

## The vision: El Experto

The insight was clear enough to build a design direction around it. We called it El Experto (The Expert): a contextual financial advisor layer embedded directly into the app's home screen.

<figure class="shot"><img src="/work/ruralvia/expert-chat.webp" alt="El Experto, a contextual advisor inside the app" loading="lazy" /><figcaption>El Experto: a contextual advisor, in the user's own words</figcaption></figure>

The idea was not a chatbot. Not a FAQ. Something closer to what happens in a good branch visit: someone who knows your situation, notices things before you do, and says something useful at the right moment.

In practice, this meant a home screen that greeted you by name and led with a proactive message from your expert, not with your balance. It meant alerts when something unusual appeared in your finances, like an electricity bill higher than usual, with a clear action attached. It meant spending visualization that didn't just show you data but helped you understand it. And at the bottom of every screen, a direct line to your real human advisor: name, photo, phone, and chat.

The configuration panel let users decide what their expert monitored and how it communicated with them. The system was smart, but the user was in control.

## A vision for the next five years

Beyond the immediate redesign, the project included a forward-looking brief: what could Ruralvía become in five years if it fully committed to its core differentiator? The Expert concept was our answer. A direction for where digital banking could go if it stayed true to what made Caja Rural different in the first place.

<figure class="shot"><img src="/work/ruralvia/validating-prototypes.webp" alt="Validating prototypes with users" loading="lazy" /><figcaption>Validating prototypes with real users</figcaption></figure>

## And the industry caught up

What we designed in 2018 is what Cleo, Revolut and N26 are building today. Proactive financial advice. Contextual alerts. A product that feels like it's working for you. The research pointed there. The vision was ready. The timing was ahead of the market.
